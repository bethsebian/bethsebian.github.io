The question is never whether change is coming.
It’s whether anyone is paying attention when it does.

I write at the intersection of technology, moral philosophy, and the lives of actual people. My work comes from a conviction that organizations — like individuals — can move through consequential moments without once stopping to ask what they're doing or why. That unreflective movement is not neutral. It carries weight.

Most of what gets called "AI governance" is a compliance exercise. I'm interested in something harder: what it takes to build genuine moral judgment at the level of the person who has to make a call on a Tuesday afternoon. That requires community, not documents.

These essays are part of that ongoing reckoning. They are speculative, theological in texture, and occasionally uncomfortable. I write them because I think clarity — earned slowly — is one of the few things worth offering.
